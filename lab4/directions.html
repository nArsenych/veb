RENAISSANCE

The Renaissance, spanning roughly from the 14th to the 17th centuries, was a pivotal period in European history that marked the transition from the medieval to the modern world. The Renaissance had a profound impact on art, literature, science, philosophy, and politics. The Renaissance laid the groundwork for the modern world, influencing the subsequent periods of the Reformation, the Enlightenment, and beyond. Its impact on art and culture can still be seen today.

BAROQUE

The Baroque period, spanning roughly from the late 16th century to the early 18th century, was a cultural and artistic movement that emerged in Europe. The term "Baroque" originally referred to an irregularly shaped pearl, suggesting something extravagant. The Baroque period left an indelible mark on the history of art and architecture, representing a time of creative exuberance, emotional intensity, and a departure from the restraint of the preceding Renaissance era.
