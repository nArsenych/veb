VAN GOGH

Vincent van Gogh, a Dutch Post-Impressionist painter, is among the most celebrated and influential figures in the history of art. Born on March 30, 1853, in Zundert, Netherlands, Van Gogh's life was marked by a passionate dedication to his craft that would later resonatewith generations to come. Van Gogh's early years were marked by a series of diverse occupations, from working as an art dealer to serving as a missionary in Belgium. His artistic journey truly began in the late 1880s when he decided to dedicate his life to painting.

EDWARD MUNCH

Edvard Munch, a Norwegian painter and printmaker, is best known for his iconic and emotionally charged work, "The Scream." Born on December 12, 1863, in Loten, Norway, Munch's life and art were deeply influenced by personal tragedies, illness, and the profound social and political changes of his time. Munch's early life was marked by the death of his mother from tuberculosis when he was just five years old. This event, along with the later deaths of his father and sister, had a lasting impact on his art, anxiety, and mortality.
